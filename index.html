SPECIAL CHICKEN ADOBO

Classic Filipino dish featuring chicken simmered in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices, resulting in tender, tangy, and savory flavors.

Chicken Adobo is a quintessential Filipino dish known for its savory and tangy flavors. It features chicken pieces marinated and simmered in a blend of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. The cooking process involves slow braising, allowing the flavors to infuse the chicken, resulting in tender and flavorful meat with a distinct sweet-sour taste. Served with rice, Chicken Adobo is a comforting and beloved Filipino favorite.

PINAKBET

Traditional vegetable dish with bitter melon, squash, okra, and more, cooked in shrimp paste, offering a flavorful and colorful medley of veggies.

Pinakbet is a traditional vegetable dish that showcases a vibrant mix of Filipino flavors. It consists of assorted vegetables such as bitter melon (ampalaya), eggplant, squash, okra, and string beans cooked together with shrimp paste (bagoong) and sometimes pork or shrimp. The vegetables are stir-fried or simmered until tender, creating a flavorful medley that highlights the natural taste of each ingredient. Pinakbet is a nutritious and flavorful dish often served as a main course.

KARE-KARE

Filipino stew made with tender meat (like oxtail), cooked in a rich peanut sauce, served with vegetables, creating a harmonious blend of savory and nutty flavors.

Kare Kare is a Filipino stew recognized for its rich peanut sauce and tender meat. Typically made with oxtail, tripe, or pork hocks cooked until tender, Kare Kare is simmered in a thick, savory sauce made from ground roasted peanuts, annatto seeds for color, and a mix of spices. Vegetables like eggplant, banana blossoms, and string beans are added, enhancing the dish's flavors. Kare Kare is commonly served with bagoong (shrimp paste) and steamed rice, offering a harmonious blend of savory and nutty flavors.

BULALO

Filipino beef soup with tender beef shanks, marrow bones, vegetables, and a rich savory broth.

Bulalo is a Filipino beef soup made with beef shanks and marrow bones, simmered for hours until tender. It typically includes corn on the cob, cabbage, potatoes, and other vegetables, creating a hearty and flavorful broth. Bulalo is known for its rich and savory taste, often enjoyed as a comforting meal.

DINUGUAN

Filipino pork stew cooked in a dark, flavorful sauce made with pig's blood, vinegar, and spices, offering bold and savory flavors.

Dinuguan, also known as "chocolate meat," is a savory Filipino stew made with pork, simmered in a thick, dark sauce primarily consisting of pig's blood, vinegar, and spices. The pork cuts, often including pork belly or offal, are cooked until tender in the flavorful and slightly tangy sauce. Dinuguan is known for its bold flavors and is often served with rice or Filipino rice cakes (puto).
